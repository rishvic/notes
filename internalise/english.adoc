= English: phrasing I want to produce unaided
:intent: internalise
:created: 2026-05-30
:updated: 2026-06-01
:tags: english, phrasing, concision

Production self-tests for English phrasing. Attempt your version aloud before
revealing it — the value is in producing it yourself, not recognising it. Where
an item drills a transformation, the revealed answer is a worked _exemplar_:
grade yourself on whether you applied the move, not on matching the wording.

[#need-action%collapsible]
.PR reply. The reviewer left eight comments; you've pushed fixes for five and explained why one can wait. For the last two, you want to tell the reviewer you'll read through them and work out which ones warrant a change. Write it as one sentence — plain verbs, name who acts, keep it concise.
====
Exemplar: "I'll go through the last two and *decide which to act on*."

*Grade on the transform, not the words.* Good = you recovered both verbs and
named who acts in a concise active sentence — the framing is free ("Let me read
through the last two and decide which to act on" passes just as well). A miss is
the noun-stacked, agentless version: "I'll figure out what decisions need action
on the last two," which buries *decide* (in "decisions") and *act* (in "action")
and blurs who does what.

The move: recover the buried verb and name who acts. See
xref:../reference/english-writing-pitfalls.adoc#buried-verbs[buried verbs (zombie nouns)].
====

[#which-vs-what%collapsible]
.Two messages to a colleague. (a) Three rollback options are already written up and you want them to pick one. (b) The outage's cause is still unknown and you want their theories. For each, produce the interrogative word — "which" or "what" — that fits, then state in one line the rule that decides it.
====
Answer: (a) *which* — "Which of the three rollback options do you want?" (b)
*what* — "What do you think caused the outage?"

Rule: *which* selects from a *closed, known set* (the candidates are already on
the table); *what* opens onto an *unbounded field*. Test: if you can point at the
candidates, it's _which_; if the answer ranges over open possibilities, it's
_what_.

This is a fact-like item: grade on whether you picked the right word for each
scope and named the closed-set/open-field rule, not on wording. See
xref:../reference/english-writing-pitfalls.adoc#which-vs-what["which" vs "what"].
====
