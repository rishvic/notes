Production self-tests for English phrasing. Attempt the response aloud before
revealing it — the value is in producing it yourself, not recognising it.

[#need-action%collapsible]
.You're replying to PR review comments. You've made changes for some findings and justified deferring others, and you want to say what you'll do with the rest. Say it cleanly.
====
"…to understand the findings and *decide which to act on*."

Why this beats "…figure out what decisions need action": that phrase stacks two
buried verbs — *decide* (hidden in "decisions") and *act* (hidden in "action") —
so it reads flat and blurs who does what. The move: recover the verbs and name
who acts. See xref:../reference/english-writing-pitfalls.adoc#buried-verbs[buried verbs (zombie nouns)].
====
