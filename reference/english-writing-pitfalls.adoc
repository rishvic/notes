[#buried-verbs]
== Buried verbs (zombie nouns)

A phrase that stacks abstract nouns ending in `-tion`, `-sion`, `-ment`, `-ance`
reads flat and hides who does what. Each such noun is usually a verb in
disguise: *decision* ← decide, *action* ← act, *analysis* ← analyse.

*Fix:* recover the buried verb and name who acts.

[cols="1,1",options="header"]
|===
| Flat (noun-heavy) | Direct (verb recovered)
| "what decisions need action" | "decide what to do" / "decide which to act on"
| "we conduct an analysis of the data" | "we analyse the data"
| "make a decision" | "decide"
| "take action on" | "act on"
|===

[#which-vs-what]
== "Which" vs "what" (selecting from a set)

The choice turns on the *scope of the options*, not on register: use *which* when
you are selecting from a *closed, known set* (the candidates are already on the
table); use *what* when the field is *open or unbounded*.

*Test:* can you point at the candidates? If the set is enumerated or defined →
*which*. If the answer ranges over open possibilities → *what*.

[cols="1,1",options="header"]
|===
| Open field → *what* | Closed set → *which*
| "What should we do about the outage?" | "Which of the three rollback options do you want?"
| "What caused this?" (cause not yet known) | "Which commit caused this?" (bisected to a known list)
| "What hand do you write with?" (reads as open) | "Which hand do you write with?" (only two — left or right)
|===

In the `need-action` example, "decide *which* to act on" is the precise form
because the set is closed and named — exactly two findings; "what to act on"
would loosen it to an open field.

Drilled as a production self-test in
xref:../internalise/english.adoc#which-vs-what[the "which vs what" item].
